I'm Mahdi, also known as Sum (How to pronounce my name ). With a background in Astrophysics, Data Science, and Machine Learning, I currently serve as a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Texas at Austin, contributing to the groundbreaking HETDEX observational survey.

Recently awarded my PhD from the University of California, Riverside, I conducted research as a graduate researcher fellow at the Carnegie Observatories.

My work focuses on utilizing cutting-edge cosmological simulations and Machine Learning methodologies to model various aspects of the universe, including the distribution of galaxies, their emissions, and the intergalactic gas (known as the Lyman-alpha Forest). I actively participate in collaborations such as PRIYA simulation and LATIS observational projects.

Feel free to explore summaries of my research projects below or visit my Github accountGitHub for the open-source codes and data products I have developed.

My Research

Protoclusters and protogroups in 3D Ly-alpha tomographies

In this paper we forecast the capability of the current generation of 3D Lyα tomographies (e.g. LATIS collaboration) for detecting progenitors of galaxy clusters and groups at cosmic noon with important applications in the environmental galaxy evolution.

A population of ultraviolet-dim protoclusters detected in absorption

In our collaboration (i.e. LATIS), we discovered that the largest observed galaxy protoclusters host only very few UV-selected galaxies compared to the dark matter (DM) halo abundace predicted by our mock-observations. These (findings) will provide new insights about galaxy formation in the extremest regions in the universe at cosmic noon.

Boosting Line Intensity Map Signal-to-Noise with the Ly- α Forest Cross-Correlation

In this work, we study how 3D large-scale structure observed in Ly-alpha tomographies (e.g. LATIS collaboration) can help to improve the S/N detection of upcoming molecular line intensity maps (e.g COMAP collaboration ) or to remove interloper signals contaminating the End Of Reionization explorations (e.g. TIME collaboration )
