A PhD candidate in Physics and Astronomy at University of California Riverside(UCR) and a former UCR-Carnegie graduate researcher fellow. My research is about modeling real observations through cosmological simulations. Checkout my research project summaries below or my Github accountGitHub.

My particular interests are understanding Bayesian statistics and Machine learning and how they can solve real world problems in Astrophysics and elsewhere. Optimizing Bigdata analysis is another interest of mine.

I am grateful for being part of Dr.Bird's lab at UCR and being co-advised by Dr. Newman and Dr. Rudie at Carnegie observatories on LATIS project.

Protoclusters and protogroups in 3D Ly-alpha tomographies

In our collaboration (i.e. LATIS), we discovered that the largest observed galaxy protoclusters host only very few UV-selected galaxies compared to the dark matter (DM) halo abundace predicted by our mock-observations. These (findings) will provide new insights about galaxy formation in the extremest regions in the universe at cosmic noon.

3D Ly-alpha forest tomographies and molecular line intensity map experiments

In this work (under preparation) we study how 3D large-scale structure observed in Ly-alpha tomographies (e.g. LATIS collaboration) can help to improve the S/N detection of upcoming molecular line intensity maps (e.g COMAP collaboration ) or to remove interloper signals contaminating End Of Reionization explorations (e.g. TIME collaboration )
